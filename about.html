Our Story

Vault was born out of frustration. As engineers, we'd seen the same pattern play out at every company: secrets scattered across Slack threads, outdated .env files committed to git, and that one person who "knows all the passwords" becoming a single point of failure.

When a coworker left our previous company, we spent three days tracking down all the credentials they had access to. That's when we knew there had to be a better way.

We started Vault in 2024 with a simple mission: give every engineering team the secrets infrastructure that only large companies could afford to build in-house. No more compromises between security and developer experience.

Our Values

Security by Default

We never ask users to choose between convenience and security. The secure path should always be the easy path.

Developer First

We build for developers because we are developers. If something feels clunky, we fix it until it doesn't.

Transparent Always

From our pricing to our security practices to our incident reports—we believe in being open about how we operate.

Small & Focused

We do one thing and do it well. We're not trying to be an all-in-one platform. We're building the best secrets manager, period.
